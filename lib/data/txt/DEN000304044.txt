### Background

The East Basin Road Groundwater Site [(Map)](https://semspub.epa.gov/src/document/03/2353780) consists of co-mingled groundwater plumes impacting 11 public wells within multiple wellhead protection areas owned by the Artesian and New Castle Water Company. The wells serve approximately 209,000 people and are located within the 5,000-acre area-wide investigation. The Site was proposed to EPA's National Priorities List (NPL) in September 2022 and added to the NPL in March 2023.

Several public drinking water wells have been impacted by per-and polyfluoroalkyl substances (PFAS) and volatile organic compound (VOC) contamination. The local water providers operate treatment systems that provide water to their customers that meet all State and Federal drinking water standards for regulated and unregulated contaminants. The provider’s water treatment systems have been shown to be effective in reducing the unregulated PFAS contamination concentrations. The treated water is below the proposed State of Delaware Department of Health and Social Services, Division of Public Health drinking water maximum contaminant level (MCL) for the PFAS compounds PFOA and PFOS.

El sitio de agua subterránea de East Basin Road consiste en penachos de agua subterránea mezclados que impactan en 11 pozos públicos dentro de múltiples áreas de protección de pozos propiedad de Artesian y New Castle Water Company. Los pozos sirven a aproximadamente 209,000 personas y están ubicados dentro de la investigación de 5,000 acres en toda el área.

Varios pozos públicos de agua potable se han visto afectados por la contaminación por sustancias per- y polifluoroalquilo (PFAS) y compuestos orgánicos volátiles (COV). Los proveedores de agua locales operan sistemas de tratamiento que proporcionan agua a sus clientes que cumplen con todos los estándares estatales y federales de agua potable para contaminantes regulados. Se ha demostrado que los sistemas de tratamiento de agua del proveedor son efectivos para reducir las concentraciones de PFAS.

### What Has Been Done to Clean Up the Site?

EPA and DNREC completed a preliminary assessment, site inspection, and an expanded site inspection of widespread groundwater contamination in New Castle, DE. The groundwater contamination is located in the vicinity of the New Castle County Airport, which includes the New Castle Air National Guard Base and the Duncan Readiness Center and Army Aviation Support facilities which both lease land at the airport. The State of Delaware requested EPA list the site on the NPL due to the resources available under EPA’s Superfund program, which includes the ability to be able to identify responsible parties and determine the source of groundwater contamination.

Additional information about DNREC's investigations can be found at: [https://dnrec.alpha.delaware.gov/waste-hazardous/remediation/east-basin-road/](https://dnrec.alpha.delaware.gov/waste-hazardous/remediation/east-basin-road/)

EPA y DNREC completaron una evaluación preliminar, una inspección del sitio y una inspección ampliada del sitio sobre la contaminación generalizada de las aguas subterráneas en New Castle, DE. La contaminación del agua subterránea se encuentra en las cercanías del Aeropuerto del Condado de New Castle, que incluye la Base de la Guardia Nacional Aérea de New Castle y el Centro de Preparación Duncan y las instalaciones de Apoyo a la Aviación del Ejército, que arriendan terrenos en el aeropuerto. El estado de Delaware solicitó a la EPA que incluyera el sitio en la NPL debido a los recursos disponibles bajo el programa Superfund de la EPA, que incluye la capacidad de poder identificar a las partes responsables y determinar la fuente de contaminación del agua subterránea.

### What Is the Current Site Status?

The Site was proposed to the National Priorities List (NPL) on Friday, September 9, 2022. EPA held a 60-day public comment period and accepted public comment until November 8, 2022. EPA carefully reviewed all public comments and announced the decision to add the East Basin Road Groundwater Site to the NPL on March 29, 2023. [EPA](https://semspub.epa.gov/src/document/HQ/404020) [prepared a document that explains the rationale for adding the East Basin Road Groundwater Site to the NPL](https://semspub.epa.gov/src/document/HQ/404020). Section 3 of the document includes responses to public comments. Being on the National Priorities List means the Site is a Superfund Site and is eligible for federal funding. EPA’s next step in the Superfund Process is an investigation, called a Remedial Investigation. The Remedial Investigation is a more in-depth study to determine the extent of contamination, as well as the risks posed to human health and the environment. EPA will work closely with our state counterpart, Delaware Natural Resources and Environmental Control, and the community throughout the process.
