Background
The Midessa Ground Water Plume site is located in Midland County, Texas. An unidentified source contaminated groundwater with volatile organic compounds (VOCs). EPA is working to determine the extent of the groundwater plume. Site investigations are underway to consider remedial alternatives for site contamination.
Top of Page
What Has Been Done to Clean Up the Site?
The site is being addressed through federal and state actions. The site consists of three contaminated groundwater plumes originating from an unidentified source(s). 
Top of Page
What Is the Current Site Status?
Initial actions included installation of groundwater and soil vapor monitoring wells and air sparging test wells. EPA is currently conducting a remedial investigation/feasibility study (RI/FS) for the site.
